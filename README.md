# Financial & Operational Analysis - Café and Conference Venue Dashboards

## ⚠️ Note
This repository contains a summary of a financial analysis project 
completed as part of a real-world work experience placement. The full 
report is not uploaded as it contains confidential client data. This 
README serves as a portfolio summary of the work completed.

---

## Overview
This project involved a comprehensive financial and operational analysis 
of a café and conference venue, combining quantitative and qualitative 
data to assess performance, profitability and customer perceptions. 
Tableau dashboards and Excel were used to visualise trends and integrate 
datasets, while customer surveys captured perceptions of service quality, 
menu satisfaction and venue use. The project was completed as a group 
and delivered to the client as an interactive reporting tool to support 
data-driven decision-making.

---

## What Was Done

### 1. Data Collection and Preprocessing
Multiple datasets were collected, cleaned and integrated:

- Café sales data covering two full financial years, including 
  transaction date, product group, gross sales, net sales, quantity 
  sold and wastage costs
- Conference room booking data including booking date, event name, 
  user status (internal or external) and delegate numbers
- Supporting cost datasets covering staff salaries, utilities, 
  maintenance and cleaning expenses
- Competitor data for benchmarking against two similar venues

Key financial metrics were calculated including:
- Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) = Net Sales × (1 - Gross Profit %)
- Gross Profit = Net Sales - COGS
- Conference income based on delegate numbers, DDR rates and room 
  hire charges
- Net Operating Income = Adjusted Gross Profit + Conference Income 
  - Total Operating Costs

---

### 2. Financial Analysis — Café
Year-on-year performance was compared across two financial years:

- Gross sales showed consistent growth across both financial years
- Hot drinks were the dominant profit driver, accounting for 46% 
  of total gross profit
- Daily meals generated the highest profit per unit sold
- Cake foods showed declining demand across sales, quantity and 
  profit year-on-year
- October was the most profitable month, while May showed 
  increased volume but reduced gross profit due to product mix shifts
- July recorded lower sales volume but higher gross profit driven 
  by a positive contribution from salads and sandwiches

Charts produced: bar chart for top performing categories by gross 
profit and scatter plot for cluster-based gross profit analysis 
by category.

---

### 3. Wastage Analysis
Wastage data was analysed across four dimensions - preparation 
costs, quantity discarded, retail value lost and margin impact:

- Wastage costs increased year-on-year, with a significant number 
  of units discarded representing a notable retail value loss
- The item with the highest wastage preparation cost was the 
  Cinnamon Classic
- Jacket potatoes and butter were the most wasted product by 
  quantity, with over 280 units discarded — representing the 
  highest retail loss and greatest margin reduction of any item
- A line graph tracking items sold versus wastage over time 
  revealed that jacket potatoes and butter consistently had higher 
  wastage than sales across several months
- This persistent imbalance indicated inefficiencies in preparation 
  and inventory management — suggesting these items were being 
  over-prepared relative to actual customer demand
- Disposables and dairy products also showed low profitability 
  relative to their sales volumes, representing further 
  inefficiencies in the product offering
- Findings suggested that reducing portion preparation, adjusting 
  stock ordering for high-wastage items, or removing underperforming 
  products from the menu could prevent recurring losses and improve 
  overall profitability

Chart produced: line graph tracking wastage quantity versus 
items sold over time for the highest wastage products.

---

### 4. Financial Analysis — Conference Rooms
Conference room performance was analysed for the full financial year:

- Total bookings exceeded 1,200 room events with over 27,000 
  delegates attending
- External clients accounted for nearly three-quarters of all 
  delegate numbers and the majority of total income
- The Assembly Room was the most frequently booked and profitable 
  space
- Smaller rooms achieved higher income per delegate than larger 
  spaces
- Peak delegate attendance occurred in October and November
- Internal bookings peaked in summer months while external bookings 
  dominated in autumn
- No consistent correlation was found between conference delegate 
  numbers and café sales — café revenues were largely independent 
  of conference attendance, as delegate food was included in Day 
  Delegate Rate packages

Charts produced: pie chart for source distribution and calculated 
table for room booking distribution.

---

### 5. Tableau Dashboards
Interactive Tableau dashboards were designed and built to replace 
static spreadsheet reporting. The dashboards covered:

- Year-on-year café sales and profit trends
- Product category performance and gross profit by item
- Wastage tracking over time by product
- Conference room bookings, delegate numbers and income breakdown
- Relationship between café sales and conference attendance
- Monthly and seasonal patterns across both revenue streams

---

### 6. Customer Survey Insights
An online and face-to-face survey was conducted with approximately 
100 respondents including students, staff, alumni and community members:

- Service and atmosphere rated highly at an average of 8.9 out of 10
- Menu satisfaction rated slightly lower at 7.9 out of 10
- Likelihood to recommend rated at 9.2 out of 10
- Hot drinks were the most purchased product category
- The primary reason for visiting was use as a study or workspace
- Nearly half of respondents were unaware of the free historic tours
- The majority of respondents did not follow the venue on social 
  media or subscribe to its newsletter
- Open-ended feedback highlighted requests for more plug sockets, 
  increased seating, and gluten-free and vegan menu options

---

### 7. Competitor Benchmarking
Two competitor venues were benchmarked against the venue on 
capacity, pricing and services:

| Metric | Venue A | Venue B | The Exchange |
|---|---|---|---|
| Total Capacity | 848 seats | 1,106 seats | 588 seats |
| Number of Rooms | 14 | 19 | 11 |
| DDR Pricing | ~£55pp | ~£45pp | ~£60pp |
| Online Booking | Yes | Yes | No |
| Pricing Transparency | Published | Published | On request only |

Key gaps identified: lack of transparent online pricing, no online 
booking system and limited digital marketing presence compared 
to competitors.

---

### 8. SWOT Analysis

**Strengths** — heritage building, central location, university 
association, competitive pricing versus one key competitor, 
intimate atmosphere and on-site café.

**Weaknesses** — higher pricing than one competitor, no online 
booking system, limited parking, low student awareness and 
restricted marketing independence.

**Opportunities** — enhanced digital marketing targeting students 
and young professionals, online booking system, leveraging 
heritage and civic identity, rising demand for multi-use venues.

**Threats** — strong competitor digital presence, nearby modern 
venues, pricing transparency of competitors and economic 
uncertainty affecting discretionary event spend.

---

### 9. Recommendations
Five key recommendations were proposed for the client:

- Introduce transparent VAT-inclusive DDR pricing published online
- Launch targeted marketing campaigns for students and young 
  professionals via SEO and social media
- Expand café menu to include gluten-free, vegan and healthier 
  options and add charging points and additional seating
- Develop strategic partnerships with SMEs, charities and 
  academic departments
- Maintain and improve integrated Tableau dashboards for ongoing 
  financial monitoring of café, conference and wastage data

---

## Key Takeaway
The venue demonstrated strong financial resilience and customer 
appeal across both café and conference operations. Hot drinks 
and daily meals drove café profitability while external conference 
clients provided the majority of venue income. Wastage analysis 
identified persistent inefficiencies particularly in jacket potatoes 
and butter, highlighting clear opportunities for menu optimisation 
and stock management improvements. Key opportunities also lie in 
transparent pricing, digital marketing and enhanced dashboard 
reporting to support long-term sustainability.
